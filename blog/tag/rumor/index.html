Rumor: Spin-off Jimmy

By Maia Silber ’17

Tired-looking women in sleek ponytails and dirty polo shirts wipe a speckled counter, elbows rigid and rags dry. The camera focuses on bright frosting in swirls—up close, constellation-like—Cinnabons spinning on silver trays. In the background, 1940’s-style swing music plays.The whole scene runs in black-and-white, but it’s not a flashback. It’s a flash-forward, a glimpse of Saul’s fall and a reference to the Breaking Bad quip: “If I’m lucky, best case scenario, I’m managing a Cinnabon in Omaha.”The scene targeted the pulsing pleasure-center of every television addict’s brain. For some critics wary of the spin-off tendency to thrive on crass, easy references, it raised eyebrows and lowered pens. Since we already know the fate of Saul (nee Jimmy McGill)—how the Cinnabon crumbles, so to speak—the prequel could only provide “cheap hits of fanservice.

Cromwell, sullen and overdressed in a sunlit hedge garden, lowers his chin, prompting the king’s bemusement.“Your majesty can form your own opinion,” Cromwell replies. By the next episode, he’s been elected to the king’s privy counsel.The second episode of the series, based on Hilary Mantel’s best-selling novels, aired the night that another Hillary announced her bid for the 2016 presidential race.
