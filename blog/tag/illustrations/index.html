The Early Days of Advocate Illustration: 1939-1968

By Jeannie Sui Wonders ’15

The Advocate has had a long history of complimenting its written works with illustrations. Here is a series of exquisite illustrations from the early days of this tradition.An illustration from the April 1939 issue.An illustration by Howard Turner from the April 1940 issue.An illustration of Picasso by Howard Turner from the March 1940 issue. The drawing, based on a photograph taken by Man Ray, was coupled with Turner’s critique on Picasso’s body of work. “We are not at present in much of a position to evaluate Picasso along with the great painters of the past,” says Tuner in his critique. “It remains, as always, for the future to pass judgment.”An illustration by Howard Turner from the June 1940 issue.
