television, 'The Worst'

Lily Scherlis


My family had a room that was referred to exclusively as the Den. It was notable for three reasons. It contained the only couch we owned that it was o.k. to spill fluids on (it was purple leather but already scarred white from the scraping of sixty different hangnails, plus the more trimmed twenty of our parents). That couch was in that same place prior to my conception and has not moved in twenty-one years. Imagine standing still this whole time.

There was also, depending on exactly when we are talking about, any given one of the three different televisions of those two decades. I would say each one lasted almost exactly seven years, but the one in the middle (a large silver cube with a perfectly square button) is to this day the only one I knew how to operate.

There were also the two masks my parents brought back from Papua New-Guinea. All I know about that six-month escapade is that everyone referred to my mother as “Misses Doctor Bill.” My father (Bill) is not a doctor, and the trip was Mom's work assignment. Mom is a journalist. When I watched TV in there the masks would hang around my peripheral vision.

In 2010 a second television was added to the house. You could say we are now a “Two-TV Family.” What I was really concerned about at the time was that my dad’s teal Volvo S70 had dropped dead as a stone, following the white Volkswagen convertible my mother had driven just out of college (at age four we would roll up to preschool with me in the front seat of the convertible, top down, standing up with my sunglasses dangling off my face), and now every car I had ever/would ever love had been scrapped for parts. Actually I’m not sure the parts of either of those vehicles have ever done anyone any good. I did not want another television.

Anyway with the addition of the so-called “Basement TV” the den got a little bit stiffed. The Basement TV was larger and, unlike the den, you could see the screen from any given seat on the couch. So then there was this room in our house that had once been a real part of our lives that someone, anyone, probably entered approximately once a week. Sometimes, when I am home, I take phone calls in there.
