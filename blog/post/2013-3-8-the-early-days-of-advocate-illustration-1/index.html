The Early Days of Advocate Illustration: 1939-1968

Jeannie Sui Wonders ’15

The Advocate has had a long history of complimenting its written works with illustrations. Here is a series of exquisite illustrations from the early days of this tradition.


An illustration from the April 1939 issue.


An illustration by Howard Turner from the April 1940 issue.


An illustration of Picasso by Howard Turner from the March 1940 issue. The drawing, based on a photograph taken by Man Ray, was coupled with Turner’s critique on Picasso’s body of work. “We are not at present in much of a position to evaluate Picasso along with the great painters of the past,” says Tuner in his critique. “It remains, as always, for the future to pass judgment.”




An illustration by Howard Turner from the June 1940 issue. The drawing accompanies a fiction piece titled “A Place in the Sunlamp.” A review of the 1940 issue by The Harvard Crimson comments on Turner’s process of illuminating a text: “There are certain obstacles as well as advantages which are peculiar to the short-story-illustrator. Howard Turner, I imagine, in order to produce a satisfactory illustration, must rely as much upon his ability to grasp the situation and circumstances of a story as upon his ability to manipulate a pencil.”




A self-portrait by French writer and filmmaker Jean Cocteau. Cocteau submitted this drawing for the Advocate’s 75th Anniversary issue in 1942.




“The Battle of Jericho.” A pen and ink drawing by David Hollerman in an issue from 1955.




An illustration by Richard Lyons from the Fall 1963 issue.




An illustration by Carl Pickhardt from the Fall 1965 issue.




An illustration by American novelist Francine Prose. Prose was the art editor of the Advocate at the time this was printed in the Fall 1965 issue.




An illustration by Barbara Beatty from the March 1966 issue.



The illustration for the March 1966 cover. Terry Furchgott depicts a scene from J.R.R. Tolkien’s The Fellowship of The Ring.




An illustration by Terry Furchgott for the cover of the March 1968 issue
